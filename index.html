Creative Hands

This is THE place to come and see the different types of creative mediums that my hands have dabbled in so far!
Special thanks to Studio Upstairs, Art4Space and Code Your Future for being part of my creative journey.

My Top 7 Creative Mediums I Have Tried So Far:

  1. Drawing
  2. Watercolour Painting
  3. Kirigami
  4. Textiles
  5. Ceramics
  6. Mosaics
  7. Coding!

The 3 photos above are examples of mosaic, kirigami and watercolour painting. I wish I could say they were created from my own hands - I am still learning, practicing and experimenting - Rome was not built in a day!

My Experiences From The Different Mediums:

  • Drawing: My first drawing was for an open call to submit artwork on the theme of 'Light and Dark' for a gallery cafe exhibition. My picture was chosen and was hooked after that to develop and continue drawing. My style is drawing with coloured pencils on black paper.
  • Watercolour Painting: I tried this for the first time at a 'Paint A Summer Meadow' workshop. It was one of the most relaxing and free-flowing experience, without worrying if I was doing it wrong because watercolouring is very forgiving!
  • Kirigami: This is a step-up from origami - origami is folding paper; kirigami is cutting and shaping. Cutting is the easiest part; shaping it afterwards is fiddly! Amazed though what you can create from a sheet of paper.
  • Textiles: Not only did I learn about textiles but also how to reuse/recycle: making a bag by weaving an assortment of different coloured shoelaces (!) and making a stud purse/card holder from the front end of a tie (!!)
  • Ceramics: This was my most experimental medium and one that involved the longest process: from rolling and shaping the clay to be baked, to then be glazed (clay-painted) before being baked again!
  • Mosaics: My least favourite as am not very artistic when it comes to making a design/picture from little bits of tiles, especially if they are different and random shapes and sizes!
  • Coding: This is my latest venture and currently on the Introduction to Digital course as a complete beginner. This webpage is my Step 4 assignment! Better crack on - still have 3 more steps to complete before 28th September!
